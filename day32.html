Statistics

Scatter Diagrams & Correlation
Ordinary 6 questions · 20 marks total
📊
📘 What is this topic?

SCATTER DIAGRAM: plots (x,y) pairs. CORRELATION: positive (both up), negative (one up, other down), none.

LINE OF BEST FIT: must pass through MEAN POINT (x̄,ȳ). INTERPOLATION (within range): reliable. EXTRAPOLATION (outside range): unreliable.

CORRELATION ≠ CAUSATION: a link doesn't mean one causes the other.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Ice cream sales and drownings both increase in summer — strong positive correlation — but ice cream does NOT cause drowning. Both are caused by a third factor: hot weather. Always look for lurking variables!

🔍 Worked Example
Hours x=(1,2,3,4,5,6). Scores y=(45,55,60,70,75,85). Mean point and correlation.
  1. x̄=(1+2+3+4+5+6)/6=3.5
  2. ȳ=(45+55+60+70+75+85)/6=65
  3. Mean point: (3.5,65) — line must pass through this
  4. Correlation: strong positive
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Line of best fit: NOT always through origin. MUST pass through mean point.
  • Extrapolation: predicting well beyond data range is very unreliable
  • Draw line across the WHOLE plot, not just between two specific points
📌 Key Formulae
Mean point: (x̄,ȳ) — line must pass through thisInterpolation: reliable; Extrapolation: unreliableCorrelation ≠ Causation
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Scatter diagrams: the line of best fit MUST pass through the mean point (x̄,ȳ). Mark the mean point as a cross on the diagram BEFORE drawing the line. Examiners look for this explicitly.
Q1 Scatter plot: study hours on x-axis, marks on y-axis. Points trend upward. Describe correlation. 2 marks
💡 Does one variable increase as the other increases? That's positive correlation.
✅ Answer: Strong positive correlation: as study hours increase, marks tend to increase.
Q2 Points cluster tightly in a downward-sloping pattern. Describe the correlation. 2 marks
💡 Downward slope = negative; tight cluster = strong.
✅ Answer: Strong negative correlation: as one variable increases, the other decreases closely.
Q3 Hours: 2,3,4,5,6,7,8. Scores: 55,60,65,70,75,80,85. Calculate mean point (x̄,ȳ). 3 marks
💡 The line of best fit MUST pass through the mean point.
✅ Answer: x̄=35/7=5; ȳ=490/7=70. Mean point: (5,70)
Q4 Using data from Q3, predict score for 9 hours study (if appropriate). 4 marks
💡 Draw the line of best fit through (5,70) and extend it to x=9.
✅ Answer: Pattern is linear (slope=5 per hour). At x=9, predict y≈90. (Slight extrapolation — caution needed.)
Q5 A study finds positive correlation between TV hours and exam marks. Does TV cause better results? 4 marks
💡 Think of what else might explain BOTH variables.
✅ Answer: No — correlation ≠ causation. A third variable (motivated student) may cause both.
Q6 Why would predicting a score for 20 study hours using the Q3 line be unreliable? 5 marks
💡 What happens when you predict far beyond your data range?
✅ Answer: Extrapolation: 20 hours is far outside the data range (2−8). Linear trend may break down; other factors (fatigue, sleep) would dominate.
