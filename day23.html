Day 23 of 45 · Geometry

Coordinate Geometry

Distance, Midpoint & Slope
Ordinary 6 questions · 20 marks total
📐
📘 What is this topic?

Distance: |AB|=√[(x₂−x₁)²+(y₂−y₁)²]. Midpoint: M=((x₁+x₂)/2,(y₁+y₂)/2). Slope: m=(y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁).

PARALLEL: equal slopes. PERPENDICULAR: m₁×m₂=−1 (negative reciprocal, m⊥=−1/m).

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Slope is like a hill's steepness: slope=3 means 3 steps up for every 1 right. Negative slope=downhill. Two parallel roads have equal steepness; perpendicular roads make perfect right angles.

🔍 Worked Example
A(1,2) B(7,6). Find slope, length, midpoint, perpendicular slope.
  1. Slope: (6−2)/(7−1)=4/6=2/3
  2. Length: √[36+16]=√52=2√13
  3. Midpoint: (4,4)
  4. Perp slope: −3/2
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Distance: SQUARE the differences — don't just add |Δx|+|Δy|
  • Slope must be (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁) — mixing up x and y gives wrong answer
  • Perp slope: flip fraction AND change sign. 2/3 → −3/2
📌 Key Formulae
|AB|=√[(x₂−x₁)²+(y₂−y₁)²]M=((x₁+x₂)/2,(y₁+y₂)/2)m=(y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁); m⊥=−1/m
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Coordinate geometry: perpendicular slope (−1/m) is frequently needed. Remember: perpendicular means FLIP the fraction AND CHANGE the sign. m=3/4 → m⊥=−4/3.
Q1 Find the distance between A(1,3) and B(5,7). 3 marks
💡 Apply the distance formula: √[(x₂−x₁)²+(y₂−y₁)²].
✅ Answer: |AB|=√[16+16]=√32=4√2≈5.66 units
Q2 Find the midpoint of A(1,3) and B(5,7). 2 marks
💡 Average the x-coordinates and average the y-coordinates.
✅ Answer: M=((1+5)/2,(3+7)/2)=(3,5)
Q3 Find the slope of the line through A(1,3) and B(5,7). 3 marks
💡 Slope = rise ÷ run = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁).
✅ Answer: m=(7−3)/(5−1)=4/4=1
Q4 Find the equation of the line through midpoint M from Q2, perpendicular to AB. 3 marks
💡 The perpendicular slope is −1/m. Use y−y₁=m(x−x₁).
✅ Answer: Perp slope=−1. Through (3,5): y−5=−1(x−3) → x+y−8=0
Q5 Find the equation of the line through (−1,2) with slope 3. Write as ax+by+c=0. 4 marks
💡 Use y−y₁=m(x−x₁) with (x₁,y₁)=(−1,2) and m=3.
✅ Answer: y−2=3(x+1) → 3x−y+5=0
Q6 Line l: through (1,2) and (4,5). Line k: perpendicular to l through (3,−1). Find k's equation and x-intercept. 5 marks
💡 Find slope of l, then find perpendicular slope. Use point-slope form for k.
✅ Answer: m_l=1; m_k=−1. k: y=−x+2. x-intercept at (2,0)
