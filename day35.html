Day 35 of 45 · Statistics

Probability

Counting Principles & Expected Value
Higher 6 questions · 21 marks total
📊
📘 What is this topic?

FUNDAMENTAL COUNTING PRINCIPLE: m ways × n ways = m×n ways. PERMUTATIONS (order matters): nPr=n!/(n−r)! COMBINATIONS (order doesn't matter): nCr=n!/(r!(n−r)!).

EXPECTED VALUE: E(X)=Σ[x×P(x)]. Fair game: E(X)=0.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Permutations=PASSWORDS (ABC≠CAB). Combinations=COMMITTEES (same people, different order = same committee). Use ⁿCᵣ on your calculator!

🔍 Worked Example
8 people: (a) elect president+sec+treasurer (b) form committee of 3
  1. (a) ORDER MATTERS: ⁸P₃=8×7×6=336
  2. (b) ORDER DOESN'T MATTER: ⁸C₃=336/6=56
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Always ask: does ORDER matter? Yes→Permutation. No→Combination
  • 0!=1 (by definition)
  • Negative E(X) means expected LOSS
📌 Key Formulae
n!=n×(n−1)×…×1nPr=n!/(n−r)! [order matters]nCr=n!/(r!(n−r)!) [order doesn't]E(X)=Σ[x×P(x)]
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Counting principles: ask 'does order matter?' Roles (president, secretary) → order matters → Permutation nPr. Committees/groups → order doesn't matter → Combination nCr.
Q1 How many ways can 5 students sit in a row? 2 marks
💡 5×4×3×2×1
✅ Answer: 5!=120 ways
Q2 Wardrobe: 3 shirts, 2 jumpers, 2 trousers, 2 shoes. How many outfits? 3 marks
💡 Fundamental Counting Principle: multiply all choices.
✅ Answer: 3×2×2×2=24 outfits
Q3 Choose 2 students from 6 (order doesn't matter). 3 marks
💡 ⁶C₂=6!/(2!4!)=(6×5)/(2×1)=15
✅ Answer: ⁶C₂=15 ways
Q4 From 5 candidates: elect president, secretary, treasurer. How many ways? 4 marks
💡 Different roles means order matters → Permutation.
✅ Answer: ⁵P₃=5×4×3=60 ways (order matters — different roles)
Q5 Fair die rolled. Score=value shown. Find expected value. 4 marks
💡 E(X)=Σ[x×P(x)]=1×(1/6)+2×(1/6)+…+6×(1/6)
✅ Answer: E(X)=(1+2+3+4+5+6)/6=21/6=3.5
Q6 Game: win €10(p=0.1), win €5(p=0.2), break even(p=0.3), lose €2(p=0.4). Find E(gain). Worth playing? 5 marks
💡 E(X)=10(0.1)+5(0.2)+0(0.3)+(−2)(0.4)
✅ Answer: E(X)=1+1+0−0.8=€1.20 per game. Yes, positive expected value → worth playing.
