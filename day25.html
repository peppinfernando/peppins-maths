Trigonometry

SOH CAH TOA — Right-Angled Triangles
Ordinary 6 questions · 21 marks total
📐
📘 What is this topic?

sinθ=O/H; cosθ=A/H; tanθ=O/A. Hypotenuse=always opposite right angle. Opposite and adjacent are defined relative to the angle you are working with.

To find a side: set up the ratio, solve. To find an angle: use sin⁻¹, cos⁻¹, or tan⁻¹.

Pythagoras: a²+b²=c² (right triangles only, c=hypotenuse).

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

SOH CAH TOA is a tour guide at your working angle. Looking from there: the side opposite is OPPOSITE; the side next to the guide (not hypotenuse) is ADJACENT; the longest side across from the right angle is HYPOTENUSE.

🔍 Worked Example
8m ladder, base 3m from wall. Find angle with ground.
  1. cosθ=adjacent/hypotenuse=3/8
  2. θ=cos⁻¹(3/8)≈68.0°
  3. Check: sin68°≈0.927; opp=8×0.927≈7.42m; 3²+7.42²≈64=8² ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • ALWAYS draw a diagram first. Label O, A, H relative to the GIVEN angle
  • Hypotenuse is opposite the RIGHT ANGLE, not your working angle
  • Calculator must be in DEGREE mode, not radians
📌 Key Formulae
sinθ=O/H cosθ=A/H tanθ=O/APythagoras: a²+b²=c²θ=sin⁻¹(x), cos⁻¹(x), tan⁻¹(x)
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: SOH CAH TOA appears in every paper. Draw a labelled diagram FIRST. Calculator must be in DEGREE mode. Angle of elevation/depression is always measured from the HORIZONTAL.
Q1 Right triangle: hypotenuse 13cm, one leg 5cm. Find the other leg. 2 marks
💡 Pythagoras: a²+b²=c². Rearrange to find a.
✅ Answer: a²=13²−5²=144 → a=12 cm
Q2 Right triangle: hypotenuse 7cm, angle 40°. Find side opposite to 40°. 3 marks
💡 Which ratio uses Opposite and Hypotenuse? SOH: sinθ=O/H.
✅ Answer: sin40°=opp/7 → opp=7×0.6428≈4.50 cm
Q3 Right triangle: legs 6cm and 8cm. Find angle opposite the 8cm leg. 3 marks
💡 Which ratio uses Opposite and Adjacent? TOA: tanθ=O/A.
✅ Answer: tanθ=8/6 → θ=tan⁻¹(1.333)≈53.1°
Q4 12m ladder makes 25° with the wall. How far is the base from the wall? 4 marks
💡 Draw a diagram. The angle is with the WALL — label sides accordingly.
✅ Answer: cos25°=adj/12 → adj=12×cos25°≈10.88m
Q5 Person 40m from a 30m building. Find angle of elevation to the top. 4 marks
💡 Draw a right triangle. The 40m is adjacent and 30m is opposite.
✅ Answer: tanθ=30/40 → θ=tan⁻¹(0.75)≈36.9°
Q6 From A, angle of elevation=35°. From B (50m further), angle=20°. Find tower height. 5 marks
💡 Set up two equations using tan from each point, then solve simultaneously.
✅ Answer: h=x·tan35°=(x+50)tan20°. x(tan35−tan20)=50tan20 → h≈37.9m
