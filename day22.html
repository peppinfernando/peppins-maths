Day 22 of 45 · Geometry

Geometry

Volume & Surface Area of 3D Solids
Ordinary 6 questions · 21 marks total
📐
📘 What is this topic?

VOLUME=space inside (cm³). SURFACE AREA=total area of all faces (cm²). All formulae in Tables booklet.

COMPOSITE SOLID: calculate each part separately, then ADD volumes.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Volume is filling a container with water. Surface area is wrapping a gift — how much paper to cover every face? Completely different measurements needing different formulae.

🔍 Worked Example
Cone (r=5cm, h=12cm) on hemisphere (r=5cm). Total volume.
  1. Cone: V=⅓π(25)(12)=100π cm³
  2. Hemisphere: V=(2/3)π(125)=(250π/3) cm³
  3. Total: 100π+(250/3)π=(550/3)π≈576 cm³
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Cylinder curved SA=2πrh. Total SA=2πrh+2πr²
  • Cone slant height: l=√(r²+h²), NOT vertical height
  • Sphere: V=(4/3)πr³ — students forget the 4/3
📌 Key Formulae
Cuboid: V=lwh; SA=2(lw+lh+wh)Cylinder: V=πr²h; SA=2πr²+2πrhCone: V=⅓πr²h; l=√(r²+h²); SA=πrl+πr²Sphere: V=(4/3)πr³; SA=4πr²
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Volume: composite solid questions are common. Calculate each part separately and ADD. Cone slant height l=√(r²+h²) must be calculated using Pythagoras — it is not given directly.
Q1 Find V and SA of a cuboid: 8cm×5cm×3cm. 2 marks
💡 V=l×w×h. SA=2(lw+lh+wh).
✅ Answer: V=120 cm³; SA=2(40+24+15)=158 cm²
Q2 Cylinder: radius 4cm, height 10cm. Find V and curved SA. 3 marks
💡 V=πr²h; Curved SA=2πrh (no ends).
✅ Answer: V=160π≈502.65 cm³; Curved SA=80π≈251.33 cm²
Q3 Cone: base radius 6cm, height 8cm. Find slant height and total SA. 3 marks
💡 l=√(r²+h²). Total SA=πrl+πr².
✅ Answer: l=√(36+64)=10cm; Total SA=96π≈301.6 cm²
Q4 Sphere radius 5cm. Find V and SA. 4 marks
💡 V=(4/3)πr³; SA=4πr².
✅ Answer: V=500π/3≈523.6 cm³; SA=100π≈314.2 cm²
Q5 Cone (r=3cm, h=4cm) on hemisphere (r=3cm). Total volume. 4 marks
💡 Calculate each part separately using their respective formulae.
✅ Answer: Cone=12π; Hemisphere=18π; Total=30π≈94.25 cm³
Q6 Cylinder V=4000cm³, height=twice the radius. Find r and h (to 2 d.p.). 5 marks
💡 Write h=2r, substitute into V=πr²h and solve for r.
✅ Answer: πr²(2r)=4000→r³=2000/π→r≈8.60cm; h≈17.20cm
