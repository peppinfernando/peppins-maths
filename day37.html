Day 37 of 45 · Statistics

Statistics & Probability

Mixed Exam-Style Statistics
Mixed 4 questions · 24 marks total
📊
📘 What is this topic?

SEC exam stats questions: (a) calculate/draw; (b) use your result; (c) interpret or harder calculation.

INTERPRETATION: write 2+ sentences. State WHAT you found AND WHAT IT MEANS in context. ALWAYS cite your numbers.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Stats exam questions are a story in three chapters: (a) set the scene; (b) develop the plot; (c) the conclusion or twist. Read all three before starting chapter one!

🔍 Worked Example
SEC stats question strategy
  1. Read the ENTIRE question — all parts (a)(b)(c) — before writing anything
  2. (a): draw/calculate. Show ALL steps. Label diagrams fully.
  3. (b): look for 'hence' or 'use your answer from (a)'
  4. (c): interpretation — write in FULL SENTENCES citing your numbers
  5. Never leave any part blank — a formula alone earns method marks
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • 'Positive correlation' alone is not enough — explain in context
  • Histogram: frequency=frequency density×class width
  • P(A|B): use only the B row/column total, not the grand total
📌 Key Formulae
P(A|B)=P(A∩B)/P(B)Compare datasets: compare mean (location) AND σ or IQR (spread)Interpolation: reliable; Extrapolation: unreliable
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Statistics exam part (c) is usually interpretation. Write at least 2 sentences, name BOTH variables, and cite the specific numbers you calculated. 'Positive correlation' alone is not enough.
Q1 Heights: 68,72,75,80,63,71,74,76,82,65,70,78,73,90,69,77,75,84,72,88. (a)Mean & median. (b)Range. (c)Modal class (60−70,70−80,80−90). (d)Describe histogram. 6 marks
💡 Order data for median. For modal class, count values in each group.
✅ Answer: (a)Mean=74.6; Median=75. (b)Range=27. (c)Modal class:70−80. (d)Slightly right-skewed (high value 90).
Q2 Hours x=(2,3,4,5,6,7,8). Scores y=(55,63,70,78,81,88,94). (a)Scatter diagram. (b)Mean point. (c)Predict for 8hrs. (d)Why not predict for 15hrs? 6 marks
💡 For (b): find x̄ and ȳ. The line MUST pass through (x̄,ȳ).
✅ Answer: (a)Positive correlation. (b)(5,75.6). (c)≈90. (d)Extrapolation beyond data range.
Q3 Bag: 5R, 7B. Two without replacement. (a)P(1st red). (b)P(both red). (c)P(at least one blue). 6 marks
💡 For (c): use the complement — P(at least one blue)=1−P(both red).
✅ Answer: (a)5/12. (b)5/12×4/11=20/132=5/33. (c)1−5/33=28/33
Q4 60 students: 35 study History(H), 30 Science(S), 12 both. (a)Two-way table. (b)P(H∩S). (c)P(H∪S). (d)P(H|S). 6 marks
💡 For (d): P(H|S)=students in both÷total in S=12/30.
✅ Answer: (a)H only=23, both=12, S only=18, neither=7. (b)1/5. (c)53/60. (d)2/5
