Algebra

Factorising Expressions
Ordinary 6 questions · 18 marks total
✖️
📘 What is this topic?

FACTORISING is the reverse of expanding. Method 1 — Common Factor: find HCF of all terms, take outside. Method 2 — Trinomial: find two numbers with product=constant and sum=middle coefficient. Method 3 — Difference of Two Squares: a²−b²=(a+b)(a−b).

💡 Analogy

Factorising is like working backwards from a cake to find the original ingredients. You know the expression (the cake) — what brackets (ingredients) multiplied to make it?

🔍 Worked Example
Factorise x² + 5x − 14
  1. Need: product=−14, sum=+5
  2. Test pairs: (−2,+7): product=−14 ✓, sum=+5 ✓
  3. Factors: (x−2)(x+7)
  4. Check: x²+7x−2x−14=x²+5x−14 ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Always CHECK by expanding back out — 10 seconds, can save the question
  • Take the HIGHEST common factor, not just any
  • DoTS only works with a MINUS sign: x²+9 does NOT factorise
📌 Key Formulae
Common factor: ax+ay=a(x+y)Trinomial: x²+bx+c=(x+p)(x+q) where p+q=b, pq=cDoTS: a²−b²=(a+b)(a−b)
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Factorising: SEC examiners award marks for each correct factor. Always CHECK by expanding — takes 10 seconds and can confirm or save 4 marks.

Q1 Factorise fully: 6x² − 9x 2 marks
Your Answer
💡 Find the highest common factor of 6x² and 9x.
✅ Model Answer: 3x(2x−3)
Q2 Factorise: x² − 49 2 marks
Your Answer
💡 49=7². Use the DoTS pattern: a²−b²=(a+b)(a−b).
✅ Model Answer: (x+7)(x−7) [Difference of Two Squares]
Q3 Factorise: x² + 7x + 12 3 marks
Your Answer
💡 Find two numbers that multiply to 12 and add to 7.
✅ Model Answer: (x+3)(x+4) [product=12, sum=7: 3×4=12, 3+4=7]
Q4 Factorise: x² − 5x − 14 3 marks
Your Answer
💡 One number must be negative. Product=−14, sum=−5.
✅ Model Answer: (x−7)(x+2) [product=−14, sum=−5]
Q5 Factorise: 2x² + 7x + 3 4 marks
Your Answer
💡 Try factor pairs for 2x² and check if they give +7x when combined.
✅ Model Answer: (2x+1)(x+3)
Q6 Factorise fully: 4x² − 25 4 marks
Your Answer
💡 Both terms are perfect squares with a minus sign between them.
✅ Model Answer: (2x+5)(2x−5) [DoTS: 4x²=(2x)², 25=5²]
