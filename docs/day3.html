Day 3 of 45 · Number

Integers

Directed Numbers & Operations
Foundation 6 questions · 18 marks total
🔢
📘 What is this topic?

Integers: …−3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3,… Negative numbers sit to the LEFT of zero on the number line.

Adding a negative = moving LEFT. Subtracting a negative = moving RIGHT (like adding). Multiplication: SAME signs→POSITIVE. DIFFERENT signs→NEGATIVE.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Temperature: if it is −5°C and drops 3 more degrees you get −8°C. 'How much warmer is 4°C than −3°C?' Answer: 4−(−3)=4+3=7 degrees warmer. Subtracting a negative equals adding!

🔍 Worked Example
Evaluate 8 + (−3)×(−2) − (−1)
  1. BEMDAS — Multiply first: (−3)×(−2) = +6 [same signs]
  2. Rewrite: 8 + 6 − (−1)
  3. Subtracting negative: 8 + 6 + 1
  4. Final answer: 15
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • (−)×(−) = POSITIVE. Two negatives multiplied always give positive.
  • 5−(−3) = 5+3 = 8. NOT 5−3=2.
  • On number line: −2 is GREATER than −7 (further right)
📌 Key Formulae
(+)×(+)=(+) | (−)×(−)=(+)(+)×(−)=(−) | a−(−b)=a+b
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Directed numbers are tested inside almost every algebra question. The most penalised error: not using brackets around negative substitutions, e.g. x=−3 must become (−3)²=9.
Q1 Calculate: −7 + 3 2 marks
💡 Move 3 steps to the RIGHT of −7 on the number line.
✅ Answer: −7 + 3 = −4
Q2 Calculate: 5 − (−3) 2 marks
💡 Subtracting a negative is the same as adding.
✅ Answer: 5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8
Q3 Calculate: (−4) × (−6) 3 marks
💡 Same signs → positive result.
✅ Answer: (−4) × (−6) = +24 (same signs give positive)
Q4 Calculate: −18 ÷ 3 3 marks
💡 Negative ÷ positive = negative.
✅ Answer: −18 ÷ 3 = −6 (different signs give negative)
Q5 Evaluate: 8 + (−3) × (−2) − (−1). Use BEMDAS and show each step. 4 marks
💡 Do the multiplication first, then deal with the signs.
✅ Answer: Multiply: (−3)×(−2)=+6. Rewrite: 8+6−(−1)=8+6+1=15
Q6 Monday was −5°C. Tuesday was 8° warmer. Wednesday was 11° colder than Tuesday. Which day was coldest? 4 marks
💡 Calculate each day's temperature in order.
✅ Answer: Tue: −5+8=3°C. Wed: 3−11=−8°C. Coldest=Wednesday (−8°C).
