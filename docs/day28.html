Day 28 of 45 · Geometry

Coordinate Geometry

Exam-Style Mixed Practice
Higher 6 questions · 30 marks total
📐
📘 What is this topic?

Full exam-style coordinate geometry. Difficulty: (a)slope/distance; (b)equation of line or circle; (c)intersection or point vs circle.

Key: perp bisector of a chord passes through the CENTRE. Circle from diameter → centre=midpoint of diameter.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Coordinate geometry questions are a treasure map: (a) gives direction (slope); (b) gives the path (equation); (c) finds where the path leads (intersection, inside/outside circle).

🔍 Worked Example
A(−1,3) B(5,7). Find perp bisector. Verify it passes through midpoint.
  1. Mid M=(2,5); slope AB=2/3
  2. Perp slope=−3/2
  3. Equation: y−5=−3/2(x−2) → 3x+2y=16
  4. Verify M(2,5): 3(2)+2(5)=16 ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Perp bisector: MUST pass through midpoint AND have perpendicular slope
  • Distance from centre to point: use distance formula, not circle equation
  • x-intercept: set y=0; y-intercept: set x=0
📌 Key Formulae
Perp bisector: through midpoint, slope=−1/m_ABOutside/on/inside: compare d(centre,point) with r
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Coordinate geometry questions follow a consistent SEC pattern: (a) slope/distance/midpoint; (b) equation of line or circle; (c) intersection or inside/outside. Practice this 3-part structure.
Q1 A(1,1), B(3,5), C(0,4). Eq. of AB. Line through C perpendicular to AB. 5 marks
💡 Find slope of AB, then perpendicular slope=−1/slope of AB.
✅ Answer: AB: m=2; 2x−y−1=0. Perp through C: m=−1/2; x+2y−8=0
Q2 Find intersection D of line AB and line CD from Q1. 5 marks
💡 Solve the two line equations simultaneously.
✅ Answer: 2x−y=1 and x+2y=8. → x=2, y=3. D=(2,3)
Q3 Find |CD| and midpoint of CD where C=(0,4) and D=(2,3). 5 marks
💡 Apply distance formula and midpoint formula.
✅ Answer: |CD|=√5; midpoint=(1,3.5)
Q4 Circle: diameter endpoints (−1,1) and (3,5). Find centre, radius and equation. 5 marks
💡 Centre=midpoint of diameter. r=half the diameter length.
✅ Answer: Centre=(1,3); r=2√2; Equation:(x−1)²+(y−3)²=8
Q5 Using the circle from Q4, find any x-axis and y-axis intercepts. 5 marks
💡 Set x=0 for y-intercepts; set y=0 for x-intercepts. Check if solutions are real.
✅ Answer: No x-intercepts (discriminant<0). y-intercepts: y=3±√7≈5.65 or 0.35
Q6 Is P(4,3) inside, on, or outside the circle from Q4? Show reasoning. 5 marks
💡 Calculate distance from centre to P and compare with the radius.
✅ Answer: Distance from (1,3) to (4,3)=3. r=2√2≈2.83. Since 3>2.83, P is OUTSIDE.
