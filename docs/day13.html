Day 13 of 45 · Algebra

Algebra

Quadratic Equations
Higher 6 questions · 24 marks total
✖️
📘 What is this topic?

QUADRATIC: ax²+bx+c=0 (highest power is 2). Can have 0, 1 or 2 solutions.

Method 1 — Factorise. Method 2 — Quadratic Formula (in Tables booklet). ALWAYS rearrange to =0 first.

DISCRIMINANT: b²−4ac. >0 means 2 solutions; =0 means 1; <0 means none.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

A quadratic describes a ball thrown in the air. Solutions are when height=0 (hits ground). Two solutions=hits ground at 2 points. One=grazes ground. None=never reaches ground level.

🔍 Worked Example
Solve 2x²+3x−4=0 using the quadratic formula
  1. a=2, b=3, c=−4
  2. Discriminant: 9+32=41
  3. x=(−3±√41)/4
  4. x₁≈0.85, x₂≈−2.35
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • ALWAYS rearrange to =0 first
  • In formula: calculate √(b²−4ac) carefully
  • Negative solutions ARE valid unless context says otherwise (e.g. lengths must be positive)
📌 Key Formulae

🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Quadratic equations appear in at least 2 questions yearly. The quadratic formula is in the Tables & Formulae booklet — you must know when to use it. Check solutions by substituting back.
Q1 Solve by factorising: x² − 5x + 6 = 0 3 marks
💡 Find two numbers: product=6, sum=−5.
✅ Answer: (x−2)(x−3)=0 → x=2 or x=3
Q2 Solve by factorising: 2x² + x − 3 = 0 3 marks
💡 Try factor pairs for the 2x² term.
✅ Answer: (2x+3)(x−1)=0 → x=−3/2 or x=1
Q3 Use the quadratic formula to solve: 2x²+3x−4=0 (to 2 d.p.) 4 marks
💡 Identify a=2, b=3, c=−4 and substitute into the formula.
✅ Answer: x≈0.85 or x≈−2.35
Q4 Solve by completing the square: x²+6x+2=0 4 marks
💡 Complete the square: x²+6x = (x+3)²−9.
✅ Answer: (x+3)²=7 → x=−3±√7
Q5 kx²−4x+4=0 has equal roots. Find k. 5 marks
💡 Equal roots means the discriminant equals zero.
✅ Answer: Equal roots: b²−4ac=0 → 16−16k=0 → k=1
Q6 A rectangle has length 5m greater than its width. Area=84 m². Find dimensions. 5 marks
💡 Let width=x. Write area equation, rearrange to =0, solve.
✅ Answer: x(x+5)=84 → x²+5x−84=0 → x=7 (positive). Width=7m, length=12m
