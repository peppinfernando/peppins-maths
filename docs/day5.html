Decimals & Percentages

Conversions, Rounding & % Change
Foundation→Ordinary 6 questions · 18 marks total
🔢
📘 What is this topic?

Fraction→Decimal: divide top by bottom. Decimal→%: multiply by 100. %→Decimal: divide by 100.

Rounding: look at the digit AFTER the required place. If ≥5, round up. If ≤4, keep same.

% change = (change ÷ ORIGINAL) × 100. Multiplier for x% increase: (1+x/100). Decrease: (1−x/100).

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Two successive discounts are NOT the same as one combined discount. A 20% then 35% discount on €200 gives €104 — NOT 55% off (which would be €90). The second discount acts on an already-reduced price!

🔍 Worked Example
€200 coat reduced 20% then 35%. Final price?
  1. After 20%: €200 × 0.80 = €160
  2. After 35% (on new price): €160 × 0.65 = €104
  3. Final = €104
  4. WARNING: 20%+35%=55% → €200×0.45=€90 is WRONG!
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • % change: divide by the ORIGINAL, not the new value
  • Two successive discounts ≠ one combined discount
  • Rounding 3.745 to 2 d.p.: look at 3rd decimal (5) → round UP → 3.75
📌 Key Formulae
% Change = (Change÷Original)×100Multiplier: increase=(1+x/100); decrease=(1−x/100)Compound interest: A = P(1+r/100)ⁿ
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: % change is in every paper. Always divide by the ORIGINAL value. Two successive % changes must be applied separately — never add the percentages together.
Q1 Convert 3/8 to a decimal and a percentage. 2 marks
💡 Divide 3 by 8, then multiply by 100.
✅ Answer: 3÷8=0.375; 0.375×100=37.5%
Q2 Round 3.4756 to 2 decimal places. 2 marks
💡 Look at the THIRD decimal place to decide whether to round up or keep.
✅ Answer: Look at 3rd decimal (5): round UP → 3.48
Q3 Find 15% of €240. 3 marks
💡 Convert 15% to a decimal (0.15) then multiply.
✅ Answer: 0.15 × 240 = €36
Q4 A price rises from €200 to €225. Find the percentage increase. 3 marks
💡 Divide the CHANGE by the ORIGINAL, then multiply by 100.
✅ Answer: Change=€25; (25÷200)×100 = 12.5%
Q5 A coat costs €200. Reduced by 20% then reduced by 35% in a sale. Find the final price. 4 marks
💡 Apply each discount separately. The second discount is on the NEW price.
✅ Answer: After 20%: €200×0.80=€160. After 35%: €160×0.65=€104
Q6 €5,000 is invested at 3% compound interest per year. Find the value after 2 years. 4 marks
💡 Each year, multiply the PREVIOUS year's total by 1.03.
✅ Answer: Year 1: €5,000×1.03=€5,150. Year 2: €5,150×1.03=€5,304.50
