Day 11 of 45 · Algebra

Algebra

Solving Linear Equations & Inequalities
Ordinary 6 questions · 18 marks total
✖️
📘 What is this topic?

GOLDEN RULE: whatever you do to one side, do EXACTLY the same to the other. Inequalities: same rules EXCEPT when multiplying/dividing by a NEGATIVE — flip the inequality sign!

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

An equation is a balanced seesaw. Whatever weight you add or remove from one side must be matched on the other. The solution is the value that keeps both sides exactly equal.

🔍 Worked Example
Solve 2(x+4) = 3x − 1
  1. Expand: 2x+8=3x−1
  2. Collect x-terms: 8=x−1
  3. Add 1: 9=x → x=9
  4. Check: LHS=2(13)=26. RHS=27−1=26 ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Moving a term: always CHANGE its sign. +3x on left → −3x on right
  • Inequalities: −2x>6 → x<−3 (flip sign when dividing by negative!)
  • Always CHECK by substituting your answer back in
📌 Key Formulae
Solve: ax+b=c → x=(c−b)/aFlip inequality when ×÷ by negative'Let x=…' — translate words to algebra first
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Linear equations: the SEC always awards method marks for expanding brackets, collecting terms, and the final answer separately. Never skip showing the CHECK substitution.
Q1 Solve: 3x − 7 = 11 2 marks
💡 Add 7 to both sides, then divide by 3.
✅ Answer: 3x=18; x=6
Q2 Solve: 2(x + 4) = 3x − 1 2 marks
💡 Expand the bracket first, then collect like terms.
✅ Answer: 2x+8=3x−1; 9=x; x=9
Q3 Solve: (x−1)/3 + (x+2)/4 = 5 3 marks
💡 Multiply every term by the LCD (12) to clear the fractions.
✅ Answer: LCD=12: 4(x−1)+3(x+2)=60 → 7x+2=60 → x=58/7
Q4 Solve 2x − 5 < 9 and show on a number line. 3 marks
💡 Solve like an equation. x<7 means open circle (not included) at 7.
✅ Answer: 2x<14 → x<7. Open circle at 7, arrow pointing left.
Q5 Three times a number minus 8 equals twice the number plus 5. Find the number. 4 marks
💡 Write 'Let n = the number', then translate each phrase to algebra.
✅ Answer: 3n−8=2n+5 → n=13. Check: 39−8=31=26+5 ✓
Q6 A parent is 28 years older than their child. In 6 years, the parent will be twice the child's age. Find their current ages. 4 marks
💡 Set up equations using 'in 6 years' for both ages.
✅ Answer: Let child=c: c+34=2(c+6) → c=22. Parent=50.
