Coordinate Geometry

Equation of Line & Circle
Ordinary→Higher 6 questions · 21 marks total
📐
📘 What is this topic?

Line: y−y₁=m(x−x₁). Rearrange to y=mx+c or ax+by+c=0.

Circle: (x−h)²+(y−k)²=r² with centre (h,k) and radius r.

Point vs circle: distance from centre < r → inside; = r → on circle; > r → outside.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

The circle equation is Pythagoras in disguise. Any point on the circle is exactly r units from the centre. Distance formula gives r² on both sides after squaring — that's the circle equation.

🔍 Worked Example
Circle diameter endpoints (−2,3) and (4,7). Find equation.
  1. Centre=midpoint=((−2+4)/2,(3+7)/2)=(1,5)
  2. r=½√[(4+2)²+(7−3)²]=½√52=√13
  3. Equation: (x−1)²+(y−5)²=13
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Line: for point (3,−2) → y−(−2)=m(x−3) → y+2=m(x−3). Note double negative!
  • Circle: r is radius. If r=5, write =25 (which is r²), not =5
  • To find where circle crosses axes: set y=0 or x=0
📌 Key Formulae
Line: y−y₁=m(x−x₁)Circle: (x−h)²+(y−k)²=r²Diameter→centre=midpoint; r=½diameter length
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Circle equations: practice 'state centre and radius', 'find where circle meets axes', and 'is point inside/outside?' These three are the most common part-types in SEC questions.
Q1 Find slope and intercepts of y=2x+3. 2 marks
💡 At y-intercept, x=0. At x-intercept, y=0.
✅ Answer: Slope=2; y-int=(0,3); x-int=(−3/2,0)
Q2 Find intersection of 2x+y=7 and x−2y=1. 3 marks
💡 Substitute one equation into the other (substitution method).
✅ Answer: y=7−2x; sub: x−14+4x=1→x=3,y=1. Point (3,1)
Q3 Write equation of circle: centre (2,−3), radius 5. 3 marks
💡 Use (x−h)²+(y−k)²=r² with h=2, k=−3, r=5.
✅ Answer: (x−2)²+(y+3)²=25
Q4 Circle x²+y²=25. Find where it crosses axes. State centre and radius. 4 marks
💡 Set y=0 for x-axis crossings; set x=0 for y-axis crossings.
✅ Answer: Centre (0,0), r=5. x-axis: (±5,0). y-axis: (0,±5)
Q5 Circle (x−3)²+(y+1)²=16. Centre, radius. Is (7,−1) on circle? 4 marks
💡 Substitute the point into the circle equation. If LHS=r², it's on the circle.
✅ Answer: Centre (3,−1), r=4. Check (7,−1): (4)²+(0)²=16 ✓ On circle
Q6 Circle with diameter endpoints (−2,1) and (4,7). Find centre, radius and equation. 5 marks
💡 Centre=midpoint of diameter. Radius=half the diameter length.
✅ Answer: Centre=(1,4); r=3√2; Equation:(x−1)²+(y−4)²=18
