Trigonometry

Sine Rule, Cosine Rule & Area
Higher 6 questions · 24 marks total
📐
📘 What is this topic?

SINE RULE: a/sinA=b/sinB=c/sinC. Use for AAS, ASA.

COSINE RULE: a²=b²+c²−2bc cosA. Use for SAS (find side) or SSS (find angle). Rearranged: cosA=(b²+c²−a²)/2bc.

AREA of any triangle: ½ab sinC (C is angle BETWEEN sides a and b). All in Tables booklet.

💡 Analogy

Sine Rule: works when you have an angle opposite a known side. Cosine Rule: works when you're 'stuck' with two sides around an angle (SAS) or all three sides (SSS).

🔍 Worked Example
PQ=7, PR=5, ∠QPR=60°. Find QR and area.
  1. SAS → Cosine Rule
  2. QR²=49+25−70(0.5)=39
  3. QR=√39≈6.24cm
  4. Area=½×7×5×sin60°≈15.16 cm²
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Cosine Rule: angle A must be OPPOSITE the side a you're finding
  • Finding angle from Cosine Rule: cosA=(b²+c²−a²)/2bc → use cos⁻¹
  • Area: angle must be BETWEEN the two sides used
📌 Key Formulae
Sine Rule: a/sinA=b/sinB=c/sinCCosine Rule: a²=b²+c²−2bc cosAArea: ½ab sinC
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Sine/Cosine Rule decision: angle BETWEEN two sides and you know them → Cosine Rule. Know one angle and opposite side + another side → Sine Rule. Area=½ab sinC is in the booklet.

Q1 Triangle: ∠A=40°, ∠B=70°, b=8cm. Find a using Sine Rule. 3 marks
Your Answer
💡 Find the missing angle C first, then apply the Sine Rule.
✅ Model Answer: a/sin40=8/sin70 → a=8sin40/sin70≈5.47 cm
Q2 Triangle from Q1: find side c. 3 marks
Your Answer
💡 ∠C=180−40−70=70°. Apply Sine Rule.
✅ Model Answer: ∠C=70°; c/sin70=8/sin70 → c=8 cm (isosceles)
Q3 Triangle: PQ=7cm, PR=5cm, ∠QPR=60°. Find QR using Cosine Rule. 4 marks
Your Answer
💡 SAS: use Cosine Rule a²=b²+c²−2bc cosA where A=60°.
✅ Model Answer: QR²=49+25−70cos60=39 → QR=√39≈6.24cm
Q4 Find the area of triangle from Q3. 4 marks
Your Answer
💡 Area=½ab sinC where C is the INCLUDED angle.
✅ Model Answer: Area=½×7×5×sin60°=35√3/4≈15.16 cm²
Q5 Triangle sides: a=7, b=6, c=5. Find angle A using Cosine Rule. 5 marks
Your Answer
💡 Rearrange Cosine Rule: cosA=(b²+c²−a²)/(2bc).
✅ Model Answer: cosA=(36+25−49)/60=0.2 → A=cos⁻¹(0.2)≈78.5°

Your Answer
💡 The angle between the two paths is 110°. Apply the Cosine Rule.
✅ Model Answer: d²=144+225−360cos110≈492.1 → d≈22.2km
