🔍 Worked Example
Stats exam technique — two-way table
  1. Draw table with ROWS and COLUMNS clearly labelled
  2. Fill given numbers FIRST, then calculate missing
  3. P(A|B): use B column/row TOTAL as denominator
  4. P(A|B)=(A and B)÷(B total) — NOT the grand total
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Conditional probability: denominator=GIVEN condition total, not full sample
  • Cosine Rule: angle A must be OPPOSITE the side a you're finding
  • Volume in cm³; Surface area in cm²
📌 Key Formulae
All Geometry, Trig & Statistics formulae applyP(A|B)=P(A∩B)/P(B)

Q1 Triangle: ∠A=55°, ∠B=75°, c=12cm. (a)Find ∠C. (b)Find a (Sine Rule). (c)Find area. 10 marks
💡 (a)Angles sum to 180°. (b)a/sin55=12/sin75. (c)Area=½ac sinB.
✅ Answer: (a)∠C=50°. (b)a≈10.18cm. (c)Area≈46.8 cm²
Q2 P(−1,3), Q(3,5). (a)Slope. (b)|PQ|. (c)Perp bisector equation. (d)Intercepts of perp bisector. 10 marks
💡 Perp bisector: through midpoint M=(1,4) with perpendicular slope −2.
✅ Answer: (a)1/2. (b)2√5. (c)2x+y=6. (d)x-int(3,0); y-int(0,6)
Q3 Cone: r=6cm, h=8cm. (a)V. (b)Slant h and CSA. (c)Cone on hemisphere (same r). Total V. 10 marks
💡 (c)V_hemisphere=(2/3)πr³. Add cone V + hemisphere V.
✅ Answer: (a)96π≈301.6cm³. (b)l=10cm; CSA=60π. (c)Total=240π≈754cm³
Q4 Heights: 70,84,72,68,76,65,71,77,74,63. (a)Mean. (b)Median. (c)Q1,Q3,IQR. (d)Outliers? 10 marks
💡 Order data first: 63,65,68,70,71,72,74,76,77,84.
✅ Answer: (a)72. (b)71.5. (c)Q1=68,Q3=76,IQR=8. (d)No outliers (fences: 56 and 88).
Q5 Bag: 4R, 5B. Two drawn without replacement. (a)P(1st red). (b)P(both red). (c)P(different colours). 10 marks
💡 (c)P(different)=P(RB)+P(BR). Two possible orders.
✅ Answer: (a)4/9. (b)1/6. (c)5/9
