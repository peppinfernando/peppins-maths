Day 4 of 45 · Number

Fractions

All Four Operations with Fractions
Foundation→Ordinary 6 questions · 18 marks total
🔢
📘 What is this topic?

ADD/SUBTRACT fractions: find the LCD (lowest common denominator), convert each fraction, then combine numerators only.

MULTIPLY: straight across (numerator × numerator, denominator × denominator).

DIVIDE: flip the second fraction (find its reciprocal) and then multiply.

Always simplify your final answer.

💡 Analogy

Adding fractions without a common denominator is like adding thirds and quarters of a pizza without cutting them the same size. The LCD tells you what size to cut all slices — then you can add them fairly.

🔍 Worked Example
Calculate (3/4) ÷ (3/8)
  1. Flip second fraction: 3/8 becomes 8/3
  2. Change ÷ to ×: (3/4) × (8/3)
  3. Multiply across: 24/12
  4. Simplify: 24/12 = 2
  5. Check: how many 3/8 fit in 3/4? Answer: 2 ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • NEVER add denominators: 1/3+1/4 ≠ 2/7. Find the LCD first!
  • When multiplying: do NOT find a common denominator
  • Always simplify to lowest terms
📌 Key Formulae
a/b + c/d = (ad+bc)/bd [use LCD method](a/b)×(c/d) = ac/bd(a/b)÷(c/d) = (a/b)×(d/c)
