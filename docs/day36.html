Statistics

Standard Deviation & Normal Distribution
Higher 6 questions · 24 marks total
📊
📘 What is this topic?

STANDARD DEVIATION (σ): measures spread around the mean. Steps: (1)Find mean. (2)Subtract mean from each value. (3)Square. (4)Average. (5)Square root.

NORMAL DISTRIBUTION: bell-shaped, symmetric. 68% within ±1σ; 95% within ±2σ; 99.7% within ±3σ.

💡 Analogy

Standard deviation is measuring consistency. All scores=70% → σ=0 (perfectly consistent). Scores from 10%−100% → large σ (very inconsistent). Tight archery group=small σ.

🔍 Worked Example
Find σ for 3,5,7,9,11.
  1. Mean=7
  2. Deviations: −4,−2,0,+2,+4
  3. Squared: 16,4,0,4,16
  4. Variance=40/5=8
  5. σ=√8=2√2≈2.83
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Square each deviation SEPARATELY — deviations always sum to 0 by definition
  • Variance=σ². SD=√(Variance).
  • Normal: 68% within 1σ means BETWEEN (mean−σ) and (mean+σ)
📌 Key Formulae
σ=√[Σ(x−x̄)²/n]Normal: 68%:±1σ; 95%:±2σ; 99.7%:±3σ

✅ Model Answer: Mean=5. Dev²: 4,1,0,1,4. Variance=2. σ=√2≈1.41
Q2 Test marks: mean=70, σ=10, n=200. How many scored between 60 and 80? 3 marks
Your Answer
💡 60=70−10=mean−1σ; 80=70+10=mean+1σ. The 68% rule applies.
✅ Model Answer: 60 to 80 = within 1σ of mean = 68%. 68%×200=136 students
Q3 Using Q2, how many students scored above 90? 4 marks
Your Answer
💡 95% within 2σ means 5% outside → 2.5% above and 2.5% below.
✅ Model Answer: 90=mean+2σ. 5% outside 2σ → 2.5% above 90. 2.5%×200=5 students
