Algebra

Simultaneous Equations
Ordinary 6 questions · 27 marks total
✖️
📘 What is this topic?

SIMULTANEOUS EQUATIONS: two equations, two unknowns. Find the ONE pair of values satisfying BOTH at the same time.

ELIMINATION: multiply to match coefficients of one variable, then add/subtract to cancel it. SUBSTITUTION: rearrange one equation to 'x=…' and substitute into the other.

💡 Analogy

Simultaneous equations are like two clues in a puzzle — each alone has many answers, but combining them gives exactly ONE solution. Elimination is like cancelling one mystery by combining the clues.

🔍 Worked Example
Solve 3x+2y=12 and 5x−y=7
  1. Multiply (ii)×2: 10x−2y=14 → (iii)
  2. Add (i)+(iii): 13x=26 → x=2
  3. Substitute into (i): 6+2y=12 → y=3
  4. Check in (ii): 10−3=7 ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Elimination: for cancellation, variable signs must be OPPOSITE (then add) or equal (then subtract)
  • ALWAYS check your answer in BOTH original equations
  • Substitution: substitute into the simpler equation
📌 Key Formulae
Elimination: match coefficients → add/subtractSubstitution: rearrange one equation → substituteCheck: verify in BOTH equations
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Simultaneous equations appear every year, often in word-problem context. The elimination method is most reliable. ALWAYS verify both values in BOTH original equations.
