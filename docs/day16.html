Algebra

Algebraic Fractions
Higher 6 questions · 24 marks total
✖️
📘 What is this topic?

Algebraic fractions follow the SAME rules as number fractions. ADD/SUBTRACT: find LCD, convert, combine. SIMPLIFY: factorise top and bottom, cancel common factors. SOLVE: multiply by LCD to clear fractions.

Always check for values making denominators zero (excluded from domain).

💡 Analogy

(x²−4)/(x+2) simplifies just like 8/4=2. Factorise numerator: (x+2)(x−2). Cancel (x+2) top and bottom → (x−2). Same logic as number arithmetic, just with expressions.

🔍 Worked Example
Simplify (x²+5x+6)/(x²−4)
  1. Factorise top: (x+2)(x+3)
  2. Factorise bottom: (x+2)(x−2) [DoTS]
  3. Cancel (x+2): result is (x+3)/(x−2)
  4. Restriction: x≠2 and x≠−2
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Cancel FACTORS only (things that multiply), not TERMS (things that add)
  • Factorise denominators FIRST before finding LCD
  • After clearing fractions: check solution doesn't make any denominator zero
📌 Key Formulae
LCD: factorise denominators, take highest powersSimplify: factorise → cancelSolve: multiply by LCD → clear fractions
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Algebraic fractions appear in Higher Level. Key: factorise BOTH numerator and denominator before cancelling. Only cancel FACTORS (things that multiply), never TERMS (things that add).

Q1 Simplify: (x+1)/3 + (x−2)/2 3 marks
Your Answer
💡 Find the LCD (6), convert each fraction, then add numerators.
✅ Model Answer: LCD=6: [2(x+1)+3(x−2)]/6 = (5x−4)/6
Q2 Write as a single fraction: 1/x + 1/(x+2) 3 marks
Your Answer
💡 LCD=x(x+2). Multiply each fraction to get this denominator.
✅ Model Answer: [(x+2)+x]/[x(x+2)] = (2x+2)/(x²+2x)
Q3 Simplify: (x²−4)/(x+2) 4 marks
Your Answer
💡 Factorise x²−4 using the Difference of Two Squares.
✅ Model Answer: (x+2)(x−2)/(x+2) = x−2 (x≠−2)
Q4 Simplify: (x²+5x+6)/(x²−4) 4 marks
Your Answer
💡 Factorise both numerator and denominator fully, then cancel.
✅ Model Answer: (x+2)(x+3)/[(x+2)(x−2)] = (x+3)/(x−2)
Q5 Express as a single fraction: 3/(x+1) + 2/(x−1) 5 marks
Your Answer
💡 LCD=(x+1)(x−1). Multiply each numerator accordingly.
✅ Model Answer: [3(x−1)+2(x+1)]/[(x+1)(x−1)] = (5x+1)/(x²−1)
Q6 Solve: 3/(x−2) + 2 = 5 5 marks
Your Answer
💡 Multiply every term by (x−2) to clear the fraction.
✅ Model Answer: 3+2(x−2)=5(x−2) → 9=3x → x=3
