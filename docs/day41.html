🔍 Worked Example
Quick check — with/without replacement
  1. Bag: 4R, 6B. Draw 2 WITHOUT replacement.
  2. P(both R)=4/10×3/9=2/15
  3. WITH replacement: P(both R)=4/10×4/10=4/25
  4. Key: WITHOUT replacement — BOTH total (10→9) AND colour count (4→3) decrease
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Always ORDER data before finding median or quartiles
  • Without replacement: update BOTH total AND colour count after each draw
  • IQR=Q3−Q1 (not range, not Q2−Q1)

🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Statistics revision: the most common lost marks are: median without ordering; IQR confused with range; line of best fit not through mean point; conditional probability wrong denominator.
Q1 Mean and median of: 4, 7, 2, 9, 3, 6, 8. 2 marks
💡 Order data before finding median.
✅ Answer: Mean≈5.57; Ordered: 2,3,4,6,7,8,9. Median=6
Q2 Bag: 3R, 4G, 5Y. P(red)? 2 marks
💡 Favourable (3 red) ÷ total (12).
✅ Answer: 3/12=1/4
Q3 Fair die: P(score ≥ 4). 3 marks
💡 Count how many outcomes satisfy ≥4.
✅ Answer: P(4)+P(5)+P(6)=3/6=1/2
Q4 Two from Q2 bag (no replacement). P(both red). 3 marks
💡 After taking one red: 11 left, 2 red.
✅ Answer: 3/12×2/11=1/22
Q5 P(one red, one green) from Q2 bag (no replacement). 3 marks
💡 Two orders: RG and GR. Add both probabilities.
✅ Answer: P(RG)+P(GR)=(3/12)(4/11)+(4/12)(3/11)=2/11
Q6 Data: 3,1,4,2,3,5,2,1,3,4. Find mode, median and mean. 4 marks
💡 Order data first for median!
✅ Answer: Mode=3. Ordered: 1,1,2,2,3,3,3,4,4,5. Median=3. Mean=2.8
Q7 x=(1,2,3,4,5), y=(2,4,5,7,9). Describe correlation and find mean point. 4 marks
💡 The line of best fit must pass through the mean point.
✅ Answer: Strong positive. x̄=3, ȳ=5.4. Mean point (3,5.4)
Q8 Team wins with P=0.6. Next 2 games: P(win both), P(lose both), P(one each). 4 marks
💡 Independent events: multiply probabilities. One each=P(WL)+P(LW).
✅ Answer: P(WW)=0.36; P(LL)=0.16; P(one each)=0.48
Q9 Game: win €2(p=0.3), break even(p=0.5), lose €1(p=0.2). Expected gain. 4 marks
💡 E(X)=Σ[x×P(x)]=2(0.3)+0(0.5)+(−1)(0.2)
✅ Answer: E(X)=0.6+0−0.2=€0.40 per game
Q10 Find IQR for: 23,15,29,18,35,20,27,12,31,24. Any outliers? (Use 1.5×IQR rule) 4 marks
💡 Q1=median of lower half; Q3=median of upper half. Outlier if outside Q1−1.5×IQR or Q3+1.5×IQR.
✅ Answer: Q1=18, Q3=27. IQR=9. Fences: 4.5 and 40.5. No outliers.
