Day 6 of 45 · Number

Number Systems & Sets

N, Z, Q, R and Venn Diagrams
Ordinary 6 questions · 18 marks total
🔢
📘 What is this topic?

N={1,2,3,…} ⊂ Z={…,−2,−1,0,1,2,…} ⊂ Q={all fractions p/q, q≠0} ⊂ R={all real numbers, including irrationals like √2 and π}.

Venn diagrams show set relationships. A∪B = 'A OR B'. A∩B = 'A AND B'. Formula: |A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B|.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

The number sets are Russian nesting dolls: N fits inside Z, which fits inside Q, which fits inside R. Every natural number is also an integer, a rational number and a real number.

🔍 Worked Example
30 students: 18 study French, 20 Spanish, 12 both. How many study neither?
  1. Place 12 in the overlap (both)
  2. French only: 18−12=6
  3. Spanish only: 20−12=8
  4. Total in circles: 6+12+8=26
  5. Neither: 30−26 = 4 students
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Fill Venn diagrams: place INTERSECTION (both) FIRST, then find 'only A' and 'only B'
  • √4=2 ∈ N,Z,Q,R (rational). √5 is irrational — ∈ R only
  • Formula: |A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B| — subtract the overlap!
📌 Key Formulae
N ⊂ Z ⊂ Q ⊂ R|A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B|Complement: P(A')=1−P(A)
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Venn diagrams appear in Q6 most years. Always fill the INTERSECTION (both) first, then find 'only A' and 'only B' by subtracting. Formula |A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B| is in the Tables booklet.
Q1 State which number set(s) contain: (i) √4 (ii) √5 2 marks
💡 Is each number a whole number? A fraction? Or does it never end without repeating?
✅ Answer: √4=2 ∈ N,Z,Q,R. √5 is irrational → ∈ R only
Q2 A={1,2,3,5,7}, B={3,4,5,6}. Find A∩B. 2 marks
💡 List the elements that appear in BOTH sets.
✅ Answer: A∩B = {3,5}
Q3 Using the sets from Q2, find A∪B. 3 marks
💡 List ALL elements from both sets (no duplicates).
✅ Answer: A∪B = {1,2,3,4,5,6,7}
Q4 35 students: 20 play hockey, 23 play camogie, 8 play both. How many play at least one sport? 3 marks
💡 Use |A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B|.
✅ Answer: |H∪C|=20+23−8=35. All 35 students play at least one sport.
Q5 Draw a Venn diagram for Q4. How many play neither? 4 marks
💡 Fill in the overlap first (8), then find each circle's exclusive section.
✅ Answer: Hockey only:12, Both:8, Camogie only:15. Total=35. Neither=0.
Q6 Classify each: −3/4, 0, π, 2.5, −7 4 marks
💡 Work through each number set from innermost (N) outward.
✅ Answer: −3/4:Q,R. 0:Z,Q,R. π:R only. 2.5:Q,R. −7:Z,Q,R
