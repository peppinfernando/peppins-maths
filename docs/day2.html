Day 2 of 45 · Number

Natural Numbers

Factors, Multiples, HCF & LCM
Foundation 6 questions · 18 marks total
🔢
📘 What is this topic?

A FACTOR divides a number exactly with no remainder. For example 3 is a factor of 12 because 12÷3=4 exactly. A MULTIPLE is the result of multiplying a number by any whole number — multiples of 5 are 5, 10, 15, 20…

HCF (Highest Common Factor) = largest factor shared. LCM (Lowest Common Multiple) = smallest shared multiple. Use PRIME FACTORISATION for both.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Making identical gift bags: if you have 36 sweets and 48 chocolates, the HCF (12) tells you the MAXIMUM number of bags. The LCM tells you when two repeating schedules first coincide — like buses departing at different intervals.

🔍 Worked Example
Find HCF and LCM of 36 and 48
  1. Prime factorise 36: 2² × 3²
  2. Prime factorise 48: 2⁴ × 3
  3. HCF = LOWEST powers of shared primes: 2² × 3 = 12
  4. LCM = HIGHEST powers of all primes: 2⁴ × 3² = 144
  5. Check: 144÷36=4 ✓ and 144÷48=3 ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • HCF → LOWEST powers. LCM → HIGHEST powers. Students swap these constantly!
  • 1 is NOT a prime number (only one factor: itself)
  • List factors in pairs: 1×24, 2×12, 3×8, 4×6 — stop when the pair meets
📌 Key Formulae
HCF = product of shared primes at LOWEST powersLCM = product of ALL primes at HIGHEST powersFor two numbers: HCF × LCM = a × b
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: HCF/LCM: the most common error is confusing HCF (lowest powers) with LCM (highest powers). Prime factorisation always works and is worth showing in full.
Q1 List ALL factors of 24. 2 marks
💡 Find factor pairs: 1×24, 2×12, 3×8, 4×6...
✅ Answer: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24
Q2 Write the first five multiples of 7. 2 marks
💡 Multiply 7 by 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.
✅ Answer: 7, 14, 21, 28, 35
Q3 Find the HCF of 36 and 48 using prime factorisation. 3 marks
💡 Write each as a product of primes. Take LOWEST powers of shared primes.
✅ Answer: 36=2²×3²; 48=2⁴×3; HCF = 2²×3 = 12
Q4 Find the LCM of 36 and 48 using prime factorisation. 3 marks
💡 Take HIGHEST powers of ALL primes that appear.
✅ Answer: 36=2²×3²; 48=2⁴×3; LCM = 2⁴×3² = 144
Q5 Bus A leaves every 12 minutes, Bus B every 18 minutes. Both depart at 9:00 am. When do they next depart together? 4 marks
💡 The buses meet again after LCM minutes.
✅ Answer: LCM(12,18)=36 minutes. Next joint departure: 9:36 am
Q6 Find the HCF and LCM of 12, 18 and 30. 4 marks
💡 Factorise all three, then apply HCF and LCM rules to three numbers.
✅ Answer: 12=2²×3; 18=2×3²; 30=2×3×5. HCF=6; LCM=180
