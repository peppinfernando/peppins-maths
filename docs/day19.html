Day 19 of 45 · Geometry

Geometry

Theorems & Proofs
Ordinary 6 questions · 24 marks total
📐
📘 What is this topic?

Theorem 2: Triangle angles=180°. Theorem 3: Exterior angle=sum of 2 opposite interior angles. Theorem 6 (circle): Angle at centre=2×angle at circumference (same arc). Corollary: Angle in semicircle=90°.

A PROOF: every step justified by a named theorem, axiom or definition.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

Theorems are mathematical laws — always true. A proof is a legal argument: you must CITE the law (theorem) that justifies each step, not just assert things are true.

🔍 Worked Example
O is centre, ∠AOB=120°. Find ∠ACB (C on major arc).
  1. State: 'By Theorem 6, angle at centre=2×angle at circumference'
  2. 120=2×∠ACB
  3. ∠ACB=60°
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • EVERY step in a proof needs a justification — write the theorem name
  • Theorem 6: angle at centre and angle at circumference must subtend the SAME arc
  • Angle in semicircle=90° ONLY when chord is a DIAMETER
📌 Key Formulae
Thm 2: A+B+C=180°Thm 3: Exterior=opposite interior anglesThm 6: ∠centre=2×∠circumferenceCorollary: angle in semicircle=90°
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Circle Theorem 6 (angle at centre = 2×angle at circumference) is the most tested. Corollary (angle in semicircle = 90°) appears in nearly every SEC paper — know it cold.
Q1 State Theorem 2. Triangle has ∠A=70°, ∠B=∠C. Find ∠B. 3 marks
💡 State the theorem first, then use it to find the unknown angle.
✅ Answer: Thm 2: angles sum 180°. 70+2∠B=180 → ∠B=55°
Q2 Triangle PQR: ∠P=40°, ∠Q=65°. Find the exterior angle at R. 3 marks
💡 State Theorem 3, then add the two opposite interior angles.
✅ Answer: By Thm 3: exterior=40+65=105°
Q3 O is centre of circle. ∠ACB=35° (angle at circumference). Find ∠AOB. 4 marks
💡 Angle at centre = 2 × angle at circumference (same arc).
✅ Answer: By Thm 6: ∠AOB=2×35°=70°
Q4 PQ is a diameter of a circle. R is a point on the circumference. Find ∠PRQ. Justify. 4 marks
💡 What happens to the angle at the circumference when the chord is a diameter?
✅ Answer: By Corollary (angle in semicircle): ∠PRQ=90°
Q5 Circle: O is centre. Chord AB. Prove ∠AOB=2∠ACB where C is on the major arc. 5 marks
💡 Join CO and extend to a point D on the circle. Use isosceles triangle properties.
✅ Answer: Join CO, extend to D. OA=OC=OB (radii). △OAC isosceles: ∠OAC=∠OCA=x. Ext ∠AOD=2x. Similarly ∠BOD=2y. ∠AOB=2(x+y)=2∠ACB ✓
Q6 ABCD is a parallelogram. Prove ∠DAB=∠BCD using angle theorems. 5 marks
💡 Use the co-interior angles theorem for parallel lines.
✅ Answer: AB∥DC → co-int angles: ∠DAB+∠ADC=180°. Also AB∥DC → ∠ABC+∠BCD=180°. So ∠DAB=∠BCD. ✓
