Day 34 of 45 · Statistics

Probability

Combined Events & Tree Diagrams
Ordinary 6 questions · 24 marks total
📊
📘 What is this topic?

INDEPENDENT: P(A and B)=P(A)×P(B). DEPENDENT (without replacement): P(A then B)=P(A)×P(B|A).

TREE DIAGRAMS: MULTIPLY along branches (AND). ADD across branches for same outcome (OR). All final branches must SUM to 1.

💡 Analogy — How to picture it

A tree diagram is like a decision tree. You multiply along a PATH (each branch follows the previous). You ADD across PATHS to find total probability of reaching the same destination.

🔍 Worked Example
Bag 5R, 7B. Without replacement. P(one red, one blue).
  1. P(R then B)=(5/12)×(7/11)=35/132
  2. P(B then R)=(7/12)×(5/11)=35/132
  3. P(one of each)=70/132=35/66
  4. Check: all branches sum to 1 ✓
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Without replacement: BOTH total AND the selected colour decrease by 1
  • Tree: multiply ALONG; ADD across for same outcome
  • Conditional P(A|B): use B row/column total, NOT grand total
📌 Key Formulae
Independent: P(A and B)=P(A)×P(B)Dependent: P(A then B)=P(A)×P(B|A)Tree: ×along; +across for same outcome
🎯 SEC Exam Insight: Tree diagrams: without replacement — BOTH the total and the selected colour count must decrease after each draw. Always verify all terminal branches sum to exactly 1.
Q1 Bag: 4 red, 8 blue. Two drawn without replacement. P(both red). 3 marks
💡 Without replacement: second draw has 11 counters, only 3 red.
✅ Answer: P(RR)=4/12×3/11=12/132=1/11
Q2 Using Q1, find P(one red and one blue) in either order. 3 marks
💡 Two orders possible: RB and BR. Add both probabilities.
✅ Answer: P(RB)+P(BR)=(4/12)(8/11)+(8/12)(4/11)=64/132=16/33
Q3 Draw tree diagram for tossing fair coin twice. Find P(at least one head). 4 marks
💡 Easiest: P(at least one H)=1−P(no heads)=1−P(TT).
✅ Answer: P(TT)=0.25. P(at least one H)=1−0.25=0.75
Q4 Class of 40: 25 play sport (8 also do music), 15 do music. Find P(sport and music) and P(sport|music). 4 marks
💡 For conditional P: use the GIVEN condition as your new total (15 students do music).
✅ Answer: P(S∩M)=8/40=0.2. P(S|M)=8/15
Q5 Tennis: 60% first serves in, win 70% of those. First fault (40%): 2nd serve in 80%, win 60%. Find P(win point). 5 marks
💡 Draw a tree. Multiply along each path, then add the paths where the player wins.
✅ Answer: P(win)=0.6×0.7+0.4×0.8×0.6=0.42+0.192=0.612
Q6 Box1: 3R,2B. Box2: 4R,3B. One ball from each. P(same colour). 5 marks
💡 Two ways to get 'same colour': both red OR both blue. Find each and add.
✅ Answer: P(RR)=(3/5)(4/7)=12/35. P(BB)=(2/5)(3/7)=6/35. P(same)=18/35
