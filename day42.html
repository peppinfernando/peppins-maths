🔍 Worked Example
Multi-part exam question strategy
  1. Read ALL parts (a)(b)(c) BEFORE writing part (a)
  2. (a): foundational — expand, factorise or calculate. 2−3 steps.
  3. (b): 'hence'=USE part (a) answer. 'Or otherwise'=different method allowed.
  4. (c): hardest — applies skill in context or requires deeper reasoning.
  5. Formula FIRST, then substitute. Show EVERY algebraic step.
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Ignoring 'hence' — use your previous answer; saves time
  • Leaving parts blank — always attempt every part for method marks
  • Not checking quadratic solutions by substituting back in

Q1 (a) Evaluate: 5+3×(8−2)²÷9. (b) Price rises €150→€180. Find % increase. (c) Price falls €120→€108. Find % change. 10 marks
Your Answer
💡 (a)BEMDAS. (b)and(c) % change=(change÷original)×100.
✅ Model Answer: (a)17. (b)20% increase. (c)−10% decrease.
Q2 Factorise: (a)6x²−9x. (b)x²−3x−18. (c)2x²+5x+3. Hence solve (b) and (c). 10 marks
Your Answer
💡 For 'hence solve': set each factorised expression equal to zero.
✅ Model Answer: (a)3x(2x−3). (b)(x−6)(x+3)→x=6 or −3. (c)(2x+3)(x+1)→x=−3/2 or −1
Q3 Solve: (a)4x−5=2x+7. (b)x/2+(x+1)/3=5. (c)3x+2y=14 and 2x−y=1. 10 marks
Your Answer
💡 (b)Use LCD=6 to clear fractions. (c)Use elimination or substitution.
✅ Model Answer: (a)x=6. (b)x=28/5. (c)x=16/7, y=25/7
Q4 f(x)=x²−5x+6. (a)Find f(2). (b)Solve f(x)=0. (c)Find minimum. (d)Sketch graph. 10 marks
Your Answer
💡 (c)Minimum at x=−b/2a=5/2. (d)Show roots, vertex and y-intercept.
✅ Model Answer: (a)0. (b)x=2 or 3. (c)Min at x=2.5, f(2.5)=−0.25. (d)Parabola, roots 2 and 3.
Q5 Sequence 5,9,13,17,… (a)Tₙ and T₁₂. (b)S₂₀. (c)Is 201 a term? 10 marks
Your Answer
💡 (c)Set Tₙ=201 and solve for n. If n is a whole number, it's in the sequence.
✅ Model Answer: (a)Tₙ=4n+1; T₁₂=49. (b)S₂₀=860. (c)4n+1=201→n=50. Yes, 201 is 50th term.
